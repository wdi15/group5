WORLDWIDE EDUCATION

ABOUT

There are millions of kids around the world that don't have access to a proper education or a school. However, with your help, we can change that. We are a group of dedicated and educated citizens of first world countries who want to share our good fortune with the children who many not have access to free schooling by making this website. Thank you to our proud sponser, Brain Station Academy, for making this possible.

REASONS TO GET INVOLVED

Here Are the Facts

  1. Around 115 million primary school age children do not go to school.
  2. In developing nations, each additional year of schooling can improve a person's prospective earnings by an average of 10 percent.
  3. Public school is not free in most third-world countries. The students' families are expected to pay for the cost of books, school uniforms and teachers' salaries.
  4. In certain areas of Africa, the literacy rates for children 18 and under are less than 50 percent.
  5. Educated girls and women are more capable of giving birth to healthier children since education and awareness helps to reduce the spread of infectious diseases, such as HIV and AIDS, which can be passed from mother to child during childbirth.

United World Schools

The United World Schools' vision is to help children achieve their dreams by teaching them skills that are needed to break out of the poverty cycle. Because of the UWS, 5000 children are able to attend school. 26 schools in Cambodia created by the UWS are operating successfully, with many more projects planned. Most of these schools are relativily cheap, with schools handed over to the local government to manage as soon as they are successful and self-governing. For less than $1 per week per child, UWS changes the lives of those living in poverty forever by giving them access to free primary school.

Concern Worldwide

Some charities have bigger areas of interest, like Concern Worldwide. Concern Worldwide is an organization based in Europe that has been working towards this cause for more than 30 years. Even though their main aim is to provide secure access to education to children worldwide, they also do many things to ensure the safety and well-being of children. They have worked in many countries including Ethiopia, Malawi, Bangladesh, Kenya and Sierra Leone. They also specially focus on the education of girls and women. Not only is this group working to educate teachers and build schools, they also work with the local governments of some of the poorest countries in the world to challenge inequality, and remove barriers that prevent children from learning.

Future Sense Foundation

Future Sense Foundation (FSF) is aother program with education support. Not only can you help by donating, you can also volunteer abroad to teach English in the Himalayas, Tanzania, or Cambodia. FSF collaborates and partners with over 100 different organizations to support volunteers who work on over 100 projects, across 10 countries. Not only do they help improve education, promote child welfare, empower those with disabilities and fight poverty. They help provide financial assistance and manpower to many other organizations and charities.
